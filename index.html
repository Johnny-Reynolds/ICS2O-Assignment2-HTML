Leonard Adleman

History

Leonard Adleman was born in a Jewish family in Californa. They had orginally immigrated to United States. He grew up in San Francisco and
attended the University of California, Berkeley, where he received his BA degree in mathematics in 1968 and his Ph.D. degree in EECS in 1976. He is a member of the National Academy of Engineering and the National Academy of Sciences

Contributions

Leonard solved a seven-node instance of the Hamiltonian Graph problem, an NP-complete problem similar to the travelling salesman problem. In 2002, he and his research group managed to solve a 'nontrivial' problem using DNA computation. Currently, Adleman is working on the mathematical theory of Strata. He is a Computer Science professor at the University of Southern California.
